Video Overview:
Kenyan Red Tea and Okinawan Brown Sugar met aboard the Wahaha's merchant ship. They were sprites from the mountains and the sea, who embarked on Wahaha's whimsical voyage through 'The Realm of Culinary Delights'. Under the witness of a playful Milk sprite, a savory tale of mountain and sea unfolded. This indicated the birth of Wahaha's unique Brown Sugar Milk Tea flavor.

Product Overview

Wahaha Brown Sugar Milk Tea

A trendy and casual milk tea blend with a rich, smooth taste profile, crafted for the young urbanite. Featuring:

  • Authentic Okinawan brown sugar for a natural caramel sweetness.
  • Kenyan highland black tea for a refreshing aftertaste.
  • Premium imported milk for a creamy texture.

Available in 350ml, 400ml, and 450ml sizes, this beverage targets 15-25-year-olds in major cities, appealing especially to students and young professionals.

Themes and Creativity

I explored the unique blend of tradition and innovation in my animation showcasing Wahaha's Black Sugar Milk Tea. It presents a fantasy world where the rich taste of Okinawan black sugar and the robust flavor of Kenyan highland tea come together, creating a stylish yet soothing beverage. The animation highlights the sophisticated simplicity of enjoying a well-crafted drink through mythological colors and unique ukiyo-e style.

The background of the fancy story is told, like unfolding an ukiyo-e that the landscape rushes out of the paper. The translucent sails in three colors symbolize three raw materials. They rotate slowly and tend to overlap. Reddish brown: brown sugar; Milky white: milk; Orange-red: black tea.
The Ukiyo-e style was adopted to highlight the product's unique formula, Okinawa brown sugar.
The sprite of Kenyan Red Tea is standing on the side of the deck, looking over the sea. She closes her eyes and recalls her homeland - the highlands in Kenya.
The sprite of milk is wearing a cow-like coat and holding a bucket of milk. Her homeland is a cow pasture.
The playful sprite of Milk bounces and jumps, so that a drop of milk splashes out of the bucket and reflects a mysterious character.
He is wearing a Japanese kimono and a mask with a kanji that means 'sweet'on it. Everyone notices him through the light refracted from the sparkling milk beads..
He's the sprite of Okinawan Brown Sugar, with Sakura petals floating around him. He takes off his mask and greets others.
Ambiguous feelings are smeared across their faces, just like the afterglow.
The three-color sails symbolizing the three raw materials overlap, indicating the harmonious integration of the three raw materials. A new page of the tale is opened. The sprites are going to expand their cooperation to develop the unique Brown Sugar Milk Tea flavor.
0-08s:

The beginning, music is light and crescendoing, guiding viewers to the fascinating scene.
8-17s:

Gradually let viewers enter into the atmosphere, I layer ascending and descending melodies together, to use a bouncy melody to highlight the liveliness of The sprite of Milk. Besides that, The richness of the melody gradually increases, coinciding with the appearance of the sprite of Okinawan Brown Sugar.
17-18s:

Three sets of ascending staccato notes quickly played, hinting at the characters' budding infatuation.
18-22s:

Four sets of descending legato notes played slowly, hinting at the characters' resolution to collaborate in the upcoming journey.
23-26s:

The end, the music has a gentle conclusion, gradually fading away, hinting that the story continues.
